About Me

As a masters student in Artificial Intelligence, my journey is deeply personal. My experiences with mental health challenges, both personally and within my family, have fueled my passion for leveraging technology to improve mental health care and support systems.

Despite facing academic setbacks due to these struggles, I've found strength and purpose in my studies. I'm driven by the potential of AI to create more accessible, personalized, and effective mental health interventions.

When I'm not immersed in research or coding, I find solace in nature and solo travel. These experiences not only help me manage my own mental health but also inspire innovative approaches to my work in AI and psychology.

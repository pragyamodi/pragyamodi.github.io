  • A Multi-Input Deep Learning Graph Model to Classify for Alzheimer's

    University of London

    April 2022 - September 2022

    This study started out with an aim to build a deep learning model to classify for Alzheimer’s disease. The research evaluated various past studies to understand the gap in this field. Most past research focus on building an image classification model on MRI scans to classify for Alzheimer’s. However, in practice doctors use a lot of other information before coming to a diagnosis. Therefore, the study then aimed to build a multi-input deep learning graph model to classify for Alzheimer’s while trying to replicate the real-world diagnosis.

    The research provides brief outline of the implementation and evaluation process followed with a GitHub link which can be easily replicated. The final model built was also evaluated against model from past research and against single input models. The best graph model built has a test accuracy of 95.51% and replicates the real-world diagnosis process more than most studies currently available.

  • Effect of Digital Font Size on Free-Recall in Adults (Quantitative Research)

    Arden University

    January 2022 - April 2022

    Today, digital content is vastly used in all field. One of the main elements of most digital content is text. Various studies in the past have looked at how different styling elements of a test affect recall memory. However, the findings from these studies have been inconsistent. Furthermore, very few areas have focused on how digital text affects memory. This study aims to study the effect of digital font size on free recall in adults. The study has thirty-six participants sampled using opportunity sampling and follows a between-group designs. A gorilla experiment was built with a free recall task and each participant was assigned a font group at the beginning of the experiment. The three-font size used are ‘18pt’, ‘48pt’, and ‘78pt’. The study used one-way ANOVA to analyse the data and fount no statistically significant difference in means between the three group. This study adds to the past literature significantly as there are very few past studies looking at the impact of digital font size on free recall. Moreover, this results from this study contradict previous studies which have not looked at digital font sizes.   • Impact of gaming on Adolescents (Qualitative Research)

    Arden University

    August 2021 - October 2021

    The rise in the gaming industry and the availability of the internet globally has led to a surge in the number of gamers in the past decade. Past research have shown how parent-child relationships can affect the impact of gaming on adolescents. Therefore, this study aims to understand the impact of gaming on adolescents from a parent’s point of view. Two parents were interviewed using semi-structured interviews. The data collected from these interviews were then analysed using thematic analysis. Three superordinate themes were identified: “Factors influencing parent’s decision to allow their children to game”, “Experiences of the impact of gaming”, and “Parental measures to minimise the impact of gaming”. The research further highlights the importance of educating parents on the impact of gaming on adolescents.
